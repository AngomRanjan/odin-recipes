Chicken Manchurian Recipe

By ANGOM

Description

Chicken Manchurian is a fusion of Indian and Chinese cuisine. It is a delecious fried chicken toast in slightly sweet, hot and sour manchurian sauce.

Chicken Manchurian is a much popular dish in the Indian Sub-continent. It is a firmed favorite with kids and adults alike.

Ingredients

  • Boneless chicken breast cut into cubes 400 grams
  • Egg 1
  • Cornflour/ corn starch 6 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce 2 tablespoons
  • Oil 4 tablespoons + to deep fry
  • Ginger finely chopped 2 inch piece
  • Garlic finely chopped 8-10 cloves
  • Onion sliced 1 medium
  • Green chillies roughly sliced 3-4
  • Chicken stock 2 cups
  • Green capsicum cut into thin strips 1 medium
  • Vinegar 2 tablespoons
  • Spring onion greens finely chopped 2 stalks

Steps

  1. Mix the egg, four tablespoons cornflour, salt and one tablespoon soy sauce in a bowl. Add the chicken pieces and mix. Set aside for half an hour. Mix the remaining cornflour in one cup of water.
  2. Heat sufficient oil in a wok and deep fry the marinated chicken for two to three minutes. Drain on absorbent paper. Heat four tablespoons oil in a wok, add the ginger and garlic and stir fry for half a minute.
  3. Add the onion and green chillies and continue to stir fry for a minute. Stir in the remaining soy sauce, chicken stock and salt.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil. Stir in the cornflour mixture and cook for a minute more or until the sauce starts to thicken, stirring continuously.
  5. Add fried chicken pieces and capsicum and cook for a couple of minutes.
  6. Stir in vinegar and serve hot, garnished with spring onion greens.
